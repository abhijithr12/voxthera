Common Voice Disorders

1. Dysphonia

A disorder causing difficulty in producing vocal sounds, often leading to hoarseness.

2. Laryngitis

Inflammation of the vocal cords, causing temporary voice loss or a raspy voice.

3. Spasmodic Dysphonia

A neurological disorder that causes involuntary movements of the vocal cords, making speech difficult.

4. Vocal Cord Paralysis

A condition where one or both vocal cords lose movement, affecting voice quality and breathing.

5. Polyps & Nodules

Noncancerous growths on the vocal cords caused by excessive voice strain.

6. Stuttering

A speech disorder that disrupts the normal flow of speech with repetitions or prolongations of sounds.
